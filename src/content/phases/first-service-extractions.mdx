## Why this phase matters

m3OS proves that its microkernel direction is real by extracting at least the first visible core services out of the kernel address space. Console and input-related functionality become supervised ring-3 services, and the system demonstrates that they can fail and be restarted without rebooting the machine.

## How it works in m3OS

Phase 52 proves that the m3OS microkernel architecture is real by extracting console rendering and keyboard input handling from kernel-resident tasks into supervised ring-3 services. After this phase, the system demonstrates that user-visible subsystems can cross the ring-0 boundary, communicate through the Phase 50 IPC contract, and be restarted by the Phase 51 service manager without rebooting the machine.

## How it builds on earlier phases

This phase builds on Service Model Maturity and turns that foundation into First Service Extractions.

## Implementation outline

- Choose the first extraction targets and define their ring-3 service contracts.
- Narrow the kernel side to the minimal notification, mapping, and hardware-mediation responsibilities.
- Wire the new services into build, initrd, and service-manager configuration.
- Port console/input policy out of kernel-resident task code.
- Implement restart and reconnect behavior for the extracted services.
- Measure the boundary cost and document the new call/data flow.
- Add smoke or focused validation that proves restartability and correct behavior.
